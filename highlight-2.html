The Narrative

The Quiet Game is a short documentary centred on the Waterloo Women's Basketball team, exploring the discipline and mindset behind athletic performance.
The project aims to present athletes through a more personal lens, emphasizing that performance is built long before the game begins.

Concept

Sports content often prioritizes speed, intensity, and visible results. This project takes an alternative approach by focusing on stillness, repetition, and mental preparation.

The contrast between silence and movement became the core creative direction of the film, shaping both the visuals and pacing.

By documenting moments that are often overlooked, the documentary reframes performance as something built through consistency, discipline, and focus.

Filming took place across multiple sessions at the University of Waterloo Physical Activities Complex, capturing a combination of interviews, practice footage, and candid in-between moments.

The Execution

Production

The production focused on authenticity, using natural interactions, training sequences, and close-up details to create a grounded and immersive perspective.

Post-Production

In post-production, interviews were structured into a cohesive narrative and paired with supporting visuals and b-roll to reinforce key themes. Editing choices emphasized rhythm and contrast, allowing quieter moments to hold as much weight as action footage.
